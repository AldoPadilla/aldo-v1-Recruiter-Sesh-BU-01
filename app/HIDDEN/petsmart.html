The American Institute of Architects challenged us to elevate the perception of architects.
Strategy identified a disconnect between the emerging architects and the established ones. Our solution was to close this gap and create a community that would be equally as helpful as it was exciting. We aimed to change from within so that energy about architecture would radiate.

Change, Inside Outward

The Solution

DraftHouse was created as a physical and digital place where all architects could practice and share their craft. The physical space is a communal work space where emerging members can accrue hours for accreditation, meet others in similar tracks, and get mentorship from established architects. The digital space consists of four sections: 1) The public facing architecture publication. 2) A community and forum. 3) A visual tracking guide for accreditation progress. 4) The portfolio to showcase ones work.

The Role

My role was to find if a digital space could complement the physical space. After speaking to emerging architects we found that they could use a digital hub of their own. I created mental models, wireframed, and designed high fidelity mockups of what this could look like. The 3D and physical key mockup was also one of my jobs along with working side by side with Rob and Jesse on copy and art direction.
